# Discovery Call Script
## Project Sitel — First Sales Conversation

**Call length target:** 30–45 minutes
**Goal:** Qualify the prospect, uncover real pain, run a live demo, and get a clear next step

**Before the call:**
- Review their Trustpilot page for complaint themes
- Check LinkedIn for the CS team size
- Note any recent company news
- Have your demo number ready: [INSERT NUMBER]
- Have a blank proposal template open to fill in live

---

## OPENING (2–3 minutes)

**[After pleasantries — set the agenda immediately]**

> "Thanks so much for making time — really appreciate it. I want to make sure this is genuinely useful for you, so here's what I was thinking: I'll take about 10 minutes to understand what's actually happening with your support setup right now, and then I'd love to show you the demo live — you can literally call the number while we're on the call and see it in action. Does that work? And is there a hard stop I should know about?"

**[Why this works:]** Sets expectations, gives them agency, signals you're not going to pitch for 45 minutes.

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (10–15 minutes)

Work through these naturally — not as a checklist. Listen for the emotion behind the answers, not just the data.

### Call Volume

> "So to start — roughly how many inbound customer contacts are you handling each month? Phone, email, chat — all of it?"

> "Of those, how many are coming in on the phone specifically?"

> "And what are the main reasons people are calling? Like, if I looked at your call logs, what would the top three reasons be?"

*[Common answers: order tracking, returns, delivery issues, complaints. These are all perfect for AI.]*

### Current Setup

> "Walk me through how you're handling calls right now — is it an internal team, outsourced, or a bit of both?"

> "How many agents do you have on phones?"

> "What hours are you covering? And what happens outside those hours — do calls just go to voicemail?"

*[If they say voicemail: "And what percentage of those voicemails actually get called back?" — this often reveals huge leakage.]*

> "Are you using any helpdesk or CRM software — Gorgias, Zendesk, Freshdesk, anything like that?"

### Cost & Pain

> "Do you have a rough sense of what you're spending on customer support staffing per month — salaries, benefits, training, turnover costs?"

*[If they hesitate: "Even a ballpark — I'm trying to understand if what we do would make financial sense for you."]*

> "What's your average handle time on a call — from answer to resolution?"

> "What's your biggest frustration with how support is running right now?"

*[Listen hard here. This is the pain. Reflect it back, don't rush past it.]*

> "What happens during peak periods — Black Friday, sale events? Does the team cope?"

> "Have you had complaints about support quality recently — either direct feedback or on Trustpilot?"

### Internal Context

> "Who else is involved in decisions around your support infrastructure — is this something you own, or does it go up to [COO/CEO/Finance]?"

> "Have you looked at any AI or automation solutions before? What happened?"

*[If yes: "What put you off?" — this surfaces objections early so you can address them.]*

---

## TRANSITION TO DEMO (30 seconds)

> "This is really helpful — thank you. What I'm hearing is [BRIEF REFLECTION: e.g. 'you're handling around 2,000 calls a month, the team is stretched, and peak periods are genuinely painful']. That's exactly the scenario we built this for.

> Can I show you something live right now? We have a working demo — it's an actual AI agent on a real phone line. I want you to call it while we're on this call and just try to break it. Ask it about a missing order, try to escalate, give it a complaint. Would that be okay?"

*[If they say yes — give them the number and stay on the line. Let them experience it. Don't talk while they're calling.]*

*[After demo call:]*

> "What did you think? Honestly."

*[Listen. Don't fill the silence. Their reaction tells you everything.]*

---

## QUALIFICATION (5–10 minutes)

### Budget

> "If this made sense — if the numbers worked and you could see it solving the problem — is there budget available for something like this, or would it need to go through a formal approval process?"

*[If yes: "What sort of range are we talking? I want to make sure what I put in front of you is realistic."]*

*[If no budget: "Understood — what would need to happen for there to be budget?" or "Is this something that could come from operational savings rather than a new budget line?"]*

### Authority

> "If this was the right fit, who would need to be involved in saying yes — is that you, or would you need to loop someone else in?"

*[If there's another decision-maker: "Would it make sense to include [NAME] in the next conversation, or would you rather run the evaluation first?"]*

### Timeline

> "What does your timeline look like — is this something you'd want to move on in the next month or two, or is it more of a 'we're exploring for later in the year' situation?"

*[If urgent: "What's driving that timeline?"]*

*[If vague: "What would need to be true for this to become a priority?"]*

---

## NEXT STEPS CLOSE (3–5 minutes)

> "Based on what you've told me today, I think there's a genuinely strong case for this. Here's what I'd suggest as a next step: I'll put together a short proposal — not a generic deck, but something specific to [BRAND] that shows what we'd actually deploy, what it would handle, and what the ROI looks like against your current cost base. I can have that with you by [DATE — 2 business days max]."

> "Does that make sense as a next step?"

> "And when you receive it, what's the best way to review it — would you look at it alone first, or would it be worth getting [OTHER DECISION-MAKER] involved from the start?"

> "Let me just confirm: I'll send the proposal to [EMAIL] by [DATE]. Can we schedule a 30-minute call for [DATE + 3 DAYS] to walk through it together? That way I can answer any questions live."

*[Book the next call before you hang up. Don't say "I'll send it over and you can let me know." That's where deals go to die.]*
